173 No Duplication No Duplication Duplication is the primary enemy of a well-designed system. It represents additional work, additional risk, and additional unnecessary complexity. Duplication manifests itself in many forms. Lines of code that look exactly alike are, of course, duplication. Lines of code that are similar can often be massaged to look even more alike so that they can be more easily refactored. And duplication can exist in other forms such as duplication of implementation. For example, we might have two methods in a collection class: int size() {} boolean isEmpty() {} We could have separate implementations for each method. The isEmpty method could track a boolean, while size could track a counter.
