95 Data/Object Anti-Symmetry In both of the above cases the second option is preferable. We do not want to expose the details of our data. Rather we want to express our data in abstract terms. This is not merely accomplished by using interfaces and/or getters and setters. Serious thought needs to be put into the best way to represent the data that an object contains. The worst option is to blithely add getters and setters. Data/Object Anti-Symmetry These two examples show the difference between objects and data structures. Objects hide their data behind abstractions and expose functions that operate on that data. Data struc- ture expose their data and have no meaningful functions. Go back and read that again.
